TRIBUTE TO KIRAN BEDI

IPS Officer • Social Activist • Tennis Player

Early Life

Kiran Bedi was born on 9 June 1949 in Amritsar, Punjab. She is the second child of her parents. She completed her graduation in English Literature in 1968 and her post-graduation in Political Science in 1970.

Education and Career

She worked as a lecturer at Khalsa College and earned her B.Law and Ph.D. in 1988 and 1993 while serving as an IPS officer.

Professional Journey

Tennis Player: Kiran started tennis at age 9, won national titles, and played till the age of 30.
Indian Police Services: First woman IPS officer of India (1975). She served in Delhi and handled major protests and traffic reforms, earning the President’s Police Medal.
Political Career: Contested Delhi elections (2015), later became Lieutenant Governor of Puducherry and promoted the TEA mantra - Trust, Empowerment, Accountability.

Awards and Recognitions

  • Cadet Officer Award (1968)
  • President’s Police Medal (1979)
  • Ramon Magsaysay Award (1994)
  • United Nations Medal (2004)
  • Mother Teresa Memorial National Award (2005)
  • And many more...

Conclusion

Kiran Bedi’s life is a story of dedication, courage, and service. From breaking barriers as the first female IPS officer to reforming prisons and governance, she remains an inspiration for all Indians.
